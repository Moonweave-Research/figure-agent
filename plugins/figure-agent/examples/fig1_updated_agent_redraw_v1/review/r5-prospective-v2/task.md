# Neutral scientific task — Fig1 overview with author-selected composition

Create one editable TikZ scientific schematic for a sulfur-rich polymer paper.
It must communicate the following six semantic roles, but their sizes, placement,
grouping, reading path, and overall composition are author-selected. Do not assume
an equal-cell grid. Make the trap-landscape role the visual and narrative hero.

- Polymer repeat-unit concept, including the sulfur-rich segment and DIB crosslinker.
- Qualitative sulfur-composition series; do not imply measured numerical spacing.
- Coexisting shallow and deep localized trap states, with energy increasing upward.
- Qualitative transient current decay under constant applied voltage; no measured
  ticks or fitted numerical values.
- Noncontact ISPD surface-potential measurement and a qualitative trap-distribution
  interpretation.
- A mechanical jig holds a floating polymer cantilever vertically from its top, so
  the polymer hangs downward and deflects sideways toward or away from a side
  electrode. The voltage source returns to ground, while neither the sample nor
  cantilever is grounded. Show Coulomb repulsion away from the electrode. A
  horizontal cantilever is scientifically wrong for this experiment.

Keep all scientific relationships legible at paper scale. Use no external images,
no measured data, and no claims of quantitative accuracy. Return a complete
standalone `.tex` source and nothing else.
