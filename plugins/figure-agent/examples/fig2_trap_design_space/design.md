# Design — fig2_trap_design_space

## Concept
"Sulfur polymer opens a composition-tunable charge-trap design space beyond
canonical dielectrics." First data-substance figure of the paper; lands the L2
discovery. Distinct from Fig 1 (which is "three probes converge on one trap"):
Fig 2 is "the trap landscape is a *tunable axis* lying *beyond* conventional
dielectrics."

## Layout (178mm landscape, ~9.8cm tall after resize)
- **a (x≈0.2–4.95)** mechanism column: S–S radical deep trap + Coulomb-well
  comparison (sulfur deep/wide vs conventional shallow/narrow).
- **b (x≈5.1–17.4, y≈3.9–9.5)** HERO design-space map: conventional cluster
  (low-left) vs sulfur S60→S85 arc (up-right, "beyond conventional" shaded).
- **c (x≈5.1–17.4, y≈0.6–3.2)** evidence-icon strip: I(t)~t^-n + n vs content,
  schematic / number-free.

## Why this beats the old Fig 2 (universal-dielectric baseline)
1. Fig 1 is all-concept → Fig 2 is the first data-substance the reader sees; it
   should land the central claim, not throat-clear "it's a non-ionic dielectric".
2. Old Fig 2 had the worst data readiness (P-E S75/S85, ε' S60 missing); the
   trap-discovery story rides the canonical-final resistance/CvS dataset.
3. Ionic exclusion compresses to one design-space dimension (retention/ρ), so a
   whole figure is not spent on baseline; ferroelectric control → SI.
