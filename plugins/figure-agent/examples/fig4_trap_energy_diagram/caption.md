# Caption — fig4_trap_energy_diagram

**Figure 4 | Bimodal trap-state energy landscape of sulfur polymer (schematic).**
**a**, Energy-level diagram. Within the gap between the valence band (Ev, from S 3p
lone pairs) and the mobility edge (Ec) of the amorphous polymer (Eg ≈ 2 eV), two
distinct trap populations coexist: shallow tail states just below Ec (structural
kinks, ~0.1–0.3 eV) and deep Coulomb traps from S–S-radical defects (>1 eV).
Injected charge is captured and then released only slowly by thermal/field
(Poole–Frenkel) activation. **b**, The corresponding trap density of states g(Et),
drawn on the same energy axis, is bimodal: its shallow and deep peaks coincide in
energy with the shallow band and deep levels of panel a (dashed guides). Shallow
states are shown in blue/teal and deep states in red, consistent with Fig. 1.
Energies are illustrative and schematic; quantitative trap spectra appear in the
ISPD figure.
