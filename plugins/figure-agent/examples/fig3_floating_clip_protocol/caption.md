# Caption — fig3_floating_clip_protocol

**Figure 3 (SI/methods) | Floating-clip protocol: polarity-dependent transient
bending as a macroscopic probe of locked trapped charge.**
A qualitative applied-voltage waveform (top) defines four phases. **Pole**: with
the clip grounded, a high voltage injects charge that is captured by traps in the
polymer cantilever. **Float**: the clip is disconnected, locking the trapped
charge so it cannot leak. **+V drive**: a positive drive bias exerts a Coulomb
force (qE) on the locked charge, deflecting the cantilever. **−V drive**:
reversing the bias reverses the force, deflecting the cantilever in the opposite
direction. Because the Coulomb term (linear in field, sign-reversing) dominates
the always-attractive Maxwell stress (∝ E²), the bending is polarity-dependent —
distinguishing this mechanism from the polarity-independent space-charge bending
of prior trap-rich elastomers. The waveform and geometry are schematic; the
charge-sign convention is illustrative.
