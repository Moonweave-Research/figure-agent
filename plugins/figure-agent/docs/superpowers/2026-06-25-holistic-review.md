# figure-agent — Holistic Review (2026-06-25)

*Synthesis of 6 dimension audits + 8 adversarial verdicts + 2026 SOTA refresh + skeptic/builder humanize analyses. Where an adversarial verdict corrected an audit claim, the verdict wins and the reconciliation is noted inline.*

## 1. Executive Verdict

**figure-agent is a safety-gated, domain-idiomatic figure *editing* substrate with a best-in-class verify-and-recommend loop — not a figure generator, and not yet an autonomous editor.** Its load-bearing core (compile gates → deterministic detectors → adjudication → a drift-guarded, rollback-safe, `replace_text`-only apply engine, now reachable from the MCP loop) is real, well-tested (2570 tests, ruff-clean, zero stub debt), and matches its documented contract. But the *author* half of the loop is dead-on-arrival on real figures: every default-path candidate is a blind ≤0.10 cm coordinate nudge of the first offsettable selector, with no link to the detected defect, and the unguided `propose` front door returns zero actionable candidates on every real figure. The premium "hand" (a 12-macro component bank + a new structural-block rewrite engine) is genuinely capable but is either human-only or hardcoded to a single fixture. **Headline completeness: ~58%.** The single truth to internalize: **the tool's value is iteration *safety and throughput*, not iteration *autonomy* — and the canonical spec is right that this is by design, but the repo currently ships ~21% of its script LOC as self-diagnosed dead-weight and lets its own canonical layer doc fall three weeks behind the tree.** "0 tool defects ≠ correct figure" remains the hard floor under everything.

## 3. The Hand — can it draw, edit, reach premium?

**It cannot draw, and that is by explicit design.** `docs/quality-kernel-goal.md:35` states the plugin "does not own ... authoring prompts ... HALT/paste/resume loops." The hand for a blank figure is the human + host LLM; figure-agent contributes discipline, not a drawing capability. This caps the completeness of "a tool that draws/edits paper figures" — but it is a sound scope choice, not a defect.

**The mutation/apply engine is the genuinely solid core.** `scripts/candidates/candidate_apply.py` enforces `kind == "replace_text"` only (rejects everything else), a per-file sha256 source-drift guard, an `O_EXCL` mutation lock (`:176`), a rollback.patch emission (`:550`), an exactly-once terminal-status guard, and post-apply compile+export+status+detector recheck. A test asserts the `.tex` content is actually mutated (`tests/test_candidate_apply.py:216`). This is production-grade for bounded, human-approved edits.

**The macro bank is premium-*capable*, not just clean.** `styles/polymer-paper-preamble.sty` ships 12 parameterized macros: `GradSlab` does x/y gradient shade fills, `IsoBlock`/`IsoCharge`/`IsoConeTip` render isometric depth with hidden-edge dashing, `WavyChain` draws polymer chains with S-markers and trap dots, `BandDiagram`/`BellCurve`/`LogLogPlot` encode domain idioms. These can reach Nature-tier — but **they are human-only**: the autonomous loop's sole lever is the coordinate nudge, which can never invoke them. The premium primitives are not `replace_text`-reachable by the proposer.

**A new structural-rewrite hand exists and post-dates the 2026-06-21 audit.** `scripts/composition_families.py` introduces a `replace_semantic_block` op (marker-delimited whole-block swap, not a nudge) with hand-authored premium TikZ bodies (smoother bezier carrier-walk, anchored sparkline, breadth bracket) in `composition_family_templates.py`, wired into `bin/fig-agent:1186`. **Verdict-corrected nuance:** the audit's universal claim ("no premium edit can EVER be auto-generated") is *false* — this engine auto-generates 6 genuine premium block-rewrites on the real `fig3_resistance_mechanism` fixture. But it over-reaches in the other direction: `FAMILY_DATA` is hardcoded to one fixture's objects (`carrier_walk`/`current_sparkline`/`n_breadth`), so `compose-generate-families fig2` dies `selector_missing:carrier_walk`, and every output is stamped `apply_authority:"human_required"`. It is a per-figure hand-authored scaffold, not a reusable engine — the "amortize across figures" premise is **unproven**.

**Auto-gen is still toy-bound at the floor.** Every default-path candidate routes through `bounded_coordinate_offset.offset_first_coordinate` (`MAX_TRANSLATE_CM = 0.10`, verified live). The 5 "canonical families" all collapse to this one mechanism; `_first_offsettable_selector` picks the *first* coordinate-bearing selector, not the detector-flagged defect — a blind nudge, not a fix. B3 (the Slice-0 gating prereq) is unmet: `candidate_families.py:17` still hardcodes `SUPPORTED_PANEL="C"`.

**Net:** the hand is a human+host-LLM hand backed by a strong safety core and a premium bank it cannot autonomously reach. Real, but pre-Slice-0 on the autonomous half.

## 4. The Eye — what it catches vs. misses

**It catches a bounded *broken* vocabulary, deterministically.** The render-detector pipeline (collisions/clash/drift at L4) plus `scripts/semantic_assertions.py` give a reproducible, non-VLM, non-ID correctness *floor* — "is this figure broken?" — that does not depend on a model's taste or on SVG element IDs. This is a sound choice the generation-first literature largely skips.

**It cannot catch semantic or physics wrongness.** This is the repeated hard-won lesson: the cantilever physics defect (a horizontal tip with no deflection) survived *every* detector. Detectors catch overlap/clash/boundary; they do not know a beam should bend. **"0 tool defects ≠ correct figure"** is the institutionalized floor.

**The SVG-semantic safety locks are vacuous on every real figure — confirmed structurally, not just historically.** `dvisvgm` emits zero geometry element IDs on the TikZ export path: live grep counts fig1=461 paths/1 id, fig2=75/1, fig3_resistance=35/1, fig4=43/1 — and the single `id=` in every case is dvisvgm's content-free `<g id='page1'>` page wrapper, never a `<path>`. The geometry-truth BLOCKER loop in `svg_semantic_diff.py` (`for element_id, src_entry in sorted(src_geo.items())`) iterates an empty dict and emits zero findings. The Fréchet bound, occlusion guard, shape-signature, and render-ship divergence have **never run on a real figure** — only on the synthetic 80×80 `_volume_shading_demo` whose hand-authored path carries `id='electrode'`. This matches the 2026-06-21 "vacuous locks" finding exactly. The vacuity is *structural*: the locks were designed for hand-authored id-bearing SVGs, but the production pipeline strips all author ids (Probe A: confirmed dead, including `\special{dvisvgm:raw}` and node-names; DVI-mode escape blocked by `fontspec`/`\setmainfont{Arial}`).

**L4.5 vision critique is report-only** (host reads the build PNG, never auto-applies) — correct discipline given that ungrounded MLLM critique measured ~18% accuracy vs. author intent. **Taste is structurally human** here, and the repo is honest that it must be: `docs/figure-design-philosophy.md:9` records "autonomous taste judgment is falsified."

## 5. The Operating System — loop, layers, governance, direction

**Loop closure.** The verify half (render → detect → adjudicate → recommend → human-gate → compile/export) is fully live and disciplined. **B1 is genuinely closed** (verdict: *confirmed*): MCP `apply_candidate` (`mcp/_legacy_server.py:1524`) shells to the `fig-agent apply-candidate` CLI, derives success from inner `status == "applied"`, and surfaces `applied_with_failed_verification` distinctly with a rollback instruction. The memory claim that MCP still hard-refuses (`apply_requires_cli_opt_in`) is **stale** — that flag now exists only in docs, in zero live `.py`. The operator chain is correctly fenced: `fig_run` executes only adjudicate/compile/export/verify-only-fig_loop; `fig_driver`/`fig_queue`/`fig_closeout` are read-only; `fig_loop` is `MODE=verify-only` and rejects any git mutation.

**The author link is the break.** The default defect-driven path returns 0 candidates on every real figure (`candidates fig2 → 12× stale_detector_evidence + unknown_panel`; `fig3 → no_supported_candidate`). **Verdict-corrected:** the loop *can* close autonomously on fig1 panels A-E via the *family* path when given an explicit `--panel` (a CLI counterexample to "every fixture returns 0") — but the unguided MCP `propose_improvements` front door shells `candidates <name>` with no panel, so it returns 0 everywhere, and even the fig1 success is a blind +0.10 cm nudge of the first offsettable coordinate. The right characterization is the audit's spirit, not its literal universal: **verify-and-recommend amplifier, not autonomous author.**

**Layers coherence.** The L0-L10 model and the product identity ("quality kernel, not generator; no external vision APIs; no auto-design") agree across all four identity surfaces (`plugin.json`, README, AGENTS, SKILL). But `architecture-overview.md` has **zero mentions of the 17-script `composition_*` subsystem** (verified: `grep -c composition` = 0) — a contributor told to "recover the mental model from this one file" would miss an entire alive subsystem. Every path/line citation in the two core docs is also drifted (tree reorganized into `scripts/candidates|checks|quality|loop|svg_polish/`; MCP is now a shim → `mcp/_legacy_server.py`). Symbol names survive, so the "grep names not lines" discipline holds, but the layer doc is ~3 weeks stale.

**Governance soundness.** The "fence" is rigorously scoped to checkable *conventions* not *taste*, with a self-deleting rule ("if it is taste guidance dressed as a rule, delete it") and user-as-master / no-LLM-auto-judge / fig1-frozen invariants (`docs/figure-design-philosophy.md:7-17`). The canonical spec is exceptionally honest — it names its own dead-weight, the auto-gen ceiling, and the honesty-gate concern, and forbids relitigating falsified directions. This is **ahead of the field's culture.**

**Direction consistency — one real governance defect, reconciled.** The audit accused the `composition_*` engine (shipped 2026-06-23, ~2 days after the spec) of being the spec's explicitly-BLOCKED Slice 2. **Verdict: partially-confirmed — the identity half is *refuted*.** Canonical Slice 2 = *cross-figure component banking / amortization* (spec §8:79, "do not build the bank before the #1-amortize probe resolves it"); the shipped `composition_*` is *single-fixture scene-layout restructuring* (`grep -i "bank|reuse.*figure|amortiz|parameteriz" scripts/composition_*.py` = 0 hits). These are **different levers wearing a colliding "Slice 0/1/2/3" namespace**. So it is not "built the blocked Slice 2." But two real hygiene defects survive: (1) a **namespace collision** — two canonical specs both define Slices with incompatible meanings, the newer never cross-references the blocked gate, so a reader cannot verify the fence was respected; (2) the **#1-amortize probe gate that genuinely blocks the genuine Slice 2 was never recorded as passed or waived** in any doc (verified: only the spec + techstack-decision mention "amortize"; neither records a *result*).

**Honest dead-weight & doc-rot summary:** docs UNDER-claim more than over-claim (healthy). The exceptions: README still advertises the quarantined SVG-polish pipeline as a live first-class capability (Slice 3 unstarted); architecture-overview omits composition; the spec is *more pessimistic than the code* (it still names B3/candidate_families as the gating blocker when the live propose path already bypassed it onto `quality_patch_plan`).

## 6. Global consensus & whether you surpassed it

**The 2026 landscape is stable** (re-verified 2026-06-25 against primary arXiv sources). Pillars intact:
- **No system autonomously produces Nature-grade physics/materials schematics.** Every 2026 figure-gen system (PaperBanana/Nano-Banana-Pro, TikZilla, DeTikZify-class, TikZero) targets ML methodology diagrams, charts, or graphic design. The one new physics-adjacent system, **CircuitLM** (arXiv:2601.04505), works *only* because circuits have a discrete enumerable component grammar + netlist ground truth — exactly what continuous-field polymer/electrostatic schematics lack. It is the boundary case that *proves the rule*.
- **Topology hallucination is a measured field-wide failure** (SciFlow-Bench, arXiv:2602.09809: "visually plausible but structurally incorrect").
- **Grounded + pairwise MLLM judging works (SciVisAgentBench r=0.806 vs experts); ungrounded collapses (VisJudge-Bench r=0.429); self-revision is a dead echo chamber** (Revision Matters, arXiv:2406.18559).
- **BioRender is the lone zero-hallucination production proof — bio-only, by asset composition, physics ≈ 0.**

**Crisp positioning:**
- **Behind** on the field's *headline* axis (autonomous authoring): TikZ-synthesis models generate a whole compilable figure from a caption; figure-agent cannot generate a figure at all.
- **Level** on the iterative critique→refine *loop pattern*, compile-as-gate, and reference-grounding-as-aspiration.
- **Ahead** on epistemic discipline and safe-apply engineering.

**Reframe — "surpassing consensus on autonomous generation" is the wrong frame.** The field's objective (text → finished figure, scored by a gameable judge) is the one figure-agent's own evidence (the cantilever case, the ~18% number) shows is unsafe for scientific figures. The right frame is the **iteration-amplifier** thesis: premium = the *volume* of cheap human element-iteration, with the human as a permanent per-sub-region oracle. On *that* axis there is no published competitor.

**The ONE thing figure-agent genuinely contributes that the field lacks:** an honest, code-enforced refusal to auto-certify a scientific figure — a drift-guarded, rollback-safe, single-op (`replace_text`) apply engine with `creative_review_only` candidate authority enforced in code, not just prose. The field optimizes a judge; figure-agent refuses to trust one. This is a contribution of *restraint + engineering*, not generative capability — and it survives scrutiny framed that way.

**The ONE place the field is clearly ahead:** imitation learning from expert revision edit-ops (Revision Matters: 5,500 train / 742 test human edit trajectories → a learned revision policy that drove FID 23→10). The repo's own techstack doc names this the "sole remaining non-falsified source of taste." figure-agent has the *logging plumbing* (every `candidate_apply` is a before→after edit-op) but no dataset and no policy. The field has the dataset and the method; the repo has the intention.

## 7. The Humanize layer & 손맛

### (a) State of the layer as built — complete for its *own* narrow goal

`scripts/narrative_context.py` is exactly what its docs claim: a **read-only question-compiler**. It emits 5 hard-coded forward questions (`_HUMAN_REVIEW_QUESTIONS`, "which element is the visual hero?"), a `_MUST_NOT_INFER` list, and a `stop_boundaries` dict with 6 hard-`False` (model_calls, prompt_loop, generation_executor, source_mutation, rank_scoring, autonomous_patch_selection — verified live). It compiles questions *for a human to answer*. For its stated goal (prompt the human's eye without inferring intent), it is essentially complete. **Its limitation is that it is amnesiac:** it asks the 5 questions and stores none of the answers. The iteration log (`subregion_iteration_log.py:54`: `Iteration | Sub-region ID | Problem | Patch Summary | Result | Follow-up`) captures *what changed* but never *why*, and is parsed only for the ID column (`subregion_active_set.py`); the `Problem`/`Patch Summary` prose is read by no parser.

### (b) The user's deeper idea ("inject human thinking → natural figures") — skeptic vs. builder

**Skeptic (correct on the literal reading):** "inject reasoning so figures fall out" is the *self-revision arm* of Revision Matters — the one that collapsed into an echo chamber. Three reasons it is the same ~18% trap in a new coat: (1) the positive literature result requires the one input the user lacks — a *corpus of real human revision traces*; fig1's 손맛 is N≈20 on a single figure, not a trainable distribution. (2) "Human reasoning" not anchored to a *render the human is looking at* decays to generic best-practice — eloquent priors, not intent; the briefing already supplies the only useful intent. (3) The deepest point: **손맛 is not a property of an artifact (rule, CoT, or trace) — it is the per-figure residue of a human eye rejecting renders that do not exist until they are made.** You cannot pre-inject a judgment about an image that does not exist yet. `narrative_context.py` gets this right by *refusing* to inject.

**Builder (a fence-respecting version that survives):** the literature's *actual* positive signal is the **trajectory + the why**, not the final state. The defensible version of "inject my thinking" is **capture-and-replay of the human's own per-sub-region rationale, used as retrieval context for the next figure's human — never as a generator or ranker.** Concretely: add a `Why`/`Reader-effect` column to the existing iteration log (no new file, two CLI flags); have `narrative_context` read back answered rationale rows as advisory `prior_rationale[]` (still `read_only:True`); surface it in the candidate review packet in the existing `narrative_review_context` slot (`rank_eligible:false`, `blocking_allowed:false`). The injection target is **the next human, not the model.** This amortizes *design reasoning* the way the component bank amortizes *visual primitives*.

**Recommendation — both are right; resolve as follows.** The user's framing as stated (autonomous "thinking → figure") is a falsified trap; do not build it. The builder's reframe (captured-rationale retrieval) is the one fence-respecting version worth building, **but it is not 손맛-injection — it is iteration-amplification** (it makes the human's loop cheaper; it does not remove the human). **Ship only the capture half (the `Why` column) as a thin add-on inside Slice 1**, because Slice 1 is exactly when the rationale worth banking gets generated — capturing it then is a free byproduct. **Defer the replay half to its own slice, triggered only when ≥3 real fixtures carry rationale traces** (building replay on an empty corpus repeats the synthetic-fixture trap that burned the SVG locks). Be explicit in any doc: this is a *defensible bet aligned with the trace-signal finding*, not a measured win — there is no benchmark proving "primed human > cold human" for figures.

## 8. Top gaps & what to improve next (prioritized, deduplicated)

### BLOCKER
1. **Auto-gen produces no defect-driven candidate on real figures (B3 unmet).** Default path returns 0 on every real fig; the one working path (family, with `--panel`) emits a blind nudge unlinked to the detector defect. *Fix:* the Slice-0 spine — make detectors emit current `source_hashes` (clears `stale_detector_evidence`), build a line→panel map (clears `unknown_panel`), and drive selector choice from the specific detector defect id/geometry, not first-offsettable. **This is Slice 0 and it gates everything.** Until done, document the loop as human-fed-only.

### MAJOR
2. **Honesty gate: MCP `propose_improvements`/`next` return `success:true` on pure no-op refusals.** (Verified live: envelope `success:True, error:None` with inner `candidates:0, refusals:[no_supported_candidate]`.) Note: the *CLI* `propose` envelope has no `success` field and expresses no-op honestly via empty `operations[]` + explicit `refusals[]` — so this is an MCP-envelope-specific defect. *Fix:* demote envelope `success` to false (or add `no_op:true`) when inner candidate_set has zero candidates + non-empty refusals. Spec §6 honesty gate.
3. **No premium auto-lever (Slice 1).** The premium macro bank is not `replace_text`-reachable by the loop; the loop plateaus at "clean" exactly as the spec warns. *Fix (after Slice 0):* author the 2-3 `replace_text`-expressible TikZ premium levers fig2's sub-regions actually demand — parameterized, bounded, value-preserving.
4. **Composition does not generalize (the "amortize" premise is unproven).** `FAMILY_DATA` is one fixture's objects; `compose-generate-families fig2` dies `selector_missing`. *Fix:* either explicitly document composition as a per-figure premium scaffold, or extract object types into spec.yaml-declared selectors so a 2nd figure reuses families without editing `FAMILY_DATA`. This is the substrate question the canonical Slice 2 gate (#1-amortize) was supposed to answer first.
5. **~21% script LOC + ~14% test LOC are self-diagnosed dead-weight; Slice 3 not done.** The SVG-semantic + auto-patch machinery (svg_polish/svg_ship_gate/svg_semantic_diff/add_volume_shading/candidate_generator/quality_patch_*) is maintained-green but inert on real figures. *Verdict-corrected:* `quality_patch_*` is *not* in this dead set — it produces an applicable diff on real fig2; exclude it from the quarantine. *Fix:* move the genuinely-inert modules into `scripts/_quarantine/`, drop their tools from the MCP table, gate their tests behind a marker so the green count stops implying live guarantees. Treat as a release blocker for the next version bump.

### MINOR
6. **Doc-rot:** architecture-overview.md omits the entire `composition_*` subsystem and cites stale paths/lines; README advertises the quarantined SVG-polish pipeline as live. *Fix:* add an L3.7 Composition layer to the overview, refresh the `Files:` lists to the subdir layout, demote SVG-polish README rows to "quarantined/experimental, vacuous on real figures."
7. **Governance record:** record the #1-amortize probe result (or a conscious waiver) and resolve the "Slice 0/1/2/3" namespace collision between the two specs; update the canonical spec to mark B3 resolved-via-replacement and name `quality_patch_plan` as the live propose engine.
8. **Stale version/branch sprawl:** `plugin.json` at 0.9.3 (2026-06-07) predates the Slice-0 MCP loop + critique features; prune the unmerged `auto/*`/`codex/*` branches; finish or revert the cosmetic MCP `_handlers_*` re-export shims (the `_legacy_server` name is an unfinished-migration tell).
9. **Humanize capture (`Why` column)** — ride along with Slice 1 (see §7).

## 9. Final Evaluation

**1) Is the tool on the right track?** Yes. The thesis (iteration-amplifier with a permanent human quality gate, not an autonomous generator) is the *correct* bet given the 2026 evidence — the field's autonomous-generation axis is exactly the one the repo's own cantilever/18% findings show is unsafe for physics schematics, and no competitor ships figure-agent's restraint+safe-apply combination. The governance fence and the load-bearing kernel are sound. The track is right; the execution is mid-Slice-0 and carries cleanup debt.

**2) The single highest-leverage next move:** **finish Slice 0 — make the autonomous candidate path produce a *defect-driven* (not first-offsettable) candidate on a real figure.** Specifically: wire detectors to write current `source_hashes`, add a line→panel map, and select the edit target from the flagged defect's geometry. Everything else (Slice 1 premium levers, the rationale-capture column, composition generalization) is blocked behind or only meaningful after this. The lever already exists and works (the apply engine mutates real `.tex` at rc=0); the missing piece is feeding it a *relevant* candidate.

**3) The single most dangerous self-deception to guard against:** **believing the green test count and the rich envelopes mean the autonomous loop works on real figures.** It does not — the default path returns 0 candidates everywhere, the MCP envelope reports `success:true` on no-ops, ~21% of the code is maintained-green-but-inert, and "0 tool defects ≠ correct figure" still holds (the cantilever defect passed everything). The whole edifice can *look* complete and self-certifying while doing nothing on the figure in front of you. Keep the human as the per-sub-region gate, and keep verifying the load-bearing engineering facts (the 0-ID measurement, the propose-on-real-fig output) by hand — they are the facts the dashboards hide.
